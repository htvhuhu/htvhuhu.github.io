About me

I am Hong Huan (Noah) Nguyen, a highly motivated researcher with advanced degrees in Management and Information Technology. With a strong technical foundation and proven research experience, I aim to contribute impactful insights at the intersection of technology management and human development.

My research explores topics at the intersection of technology and human development, with a particular focus on information systems and organizational behavior, cybersecurity and privacy, leadership, and entrepreneurship.

I employ diverse research methodologies, including cross-sectional/longitudinal studies, behavioral surveys, experimental studies, exploratory research, econometrics, structural equation modeling, data crawling/web scraping, data mining, applied machine learning, AI, NLP, LLMs, bibliometrics, systematic literature reviews, and meta-analysis.

With a strong portfolio of publications and ongoing research projects, I am dedicated to advancing academic and industry knowledge in technology management and policy. My work has been published in indexed journals including Computers in Human Behavior, European Journal of Innovation Management, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, and SAGE Open; conference proceedings such as ICIS TREO 2024, PACIS 2025, and ICIS 2025.

I actively contribute to the academic community through my role as an ad-hoc journal reviewer for Information Systems Frontiers, The International Journal of Management Education, and Asian Education and Development Studies. Additionally, I serve as an ad-hoc conference reviewer for HICSS 2026 and ACIS 2024-2025.

Thank you for visiting, and I look forward to connecting and sharing insights in the fields of technology, research, and human development.
