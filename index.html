Welcome!

I’m Huan Hong Nguyen (Noah), a dedicated researcher and project leader with a strong background in Management (MBA) and Information Technology (Computer Science). My research journey is enriched by practical experience across diverse fields, and I hold a Project Management Professional (PMP®) certification, underscoring my skills in strategic project management and leadership.

Currently pursuing my Master’s in Computer Science, my research explores topics at the intersection of technology and human development, with a particular focus on information systems and cybersecurity, privacy and human-computer interaction, the future of work and human factors, as well as social networks and digital entrepreneurship.

I employ a diverse range of research methodologies, including cross-sectional studies, behavioral surveys, longitudinal studies, econometrics, and advanced techniques in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML). My toolkit encompasses tools such as SPSS, AMOS, SmartPLS, and VOSviewer, along with data analysis platforms like Python and R to support rigorous data-driven insights.

With a solid portfolio of publications and ongoing research projects, I am dedicated to advancing academic and industry knowledge in technology management and policy. I have led impactful projects on privacy law, digital entrepreneurship, and the effects of flexible work models on employee outcomes. My work has been published in journals including Computers in Human Behavior, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, and SAGE Open; conferences proceedings such as ICB2021, ICBI2022, Transportation Research Procedia, and ICIS 2024.

I have actively contributed to the academic community through my role as an ad-hoc journal reviewer for The International Journal of Management Education, where I provide rigorous peer reviews to support the quality and relevance of research in management education. Additionally, I serve as an ad-hoc conference reviewer for the Australasian Conference on Information Systems 2024 (ACIS 2024), ensuring that high standards are maintained for research presentations in the field of information systems.

Thank you for visiting, and I look forward to connecting and sharing insights in the fields of technology, research, and human development.
