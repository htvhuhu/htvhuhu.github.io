Noah Nguyen, MBA, MSc, PMP®

I am a researcher specializing in information systems, technology management, and organizational behavior. I hold advanced degrees in Management (MBA) and Information Technology (MSc), along with professional certification as a Project Management Professional (PMP®). My scholarly work bridges the domains of information systems, human behavior, and digital innovation.

My research agenda focuses on the socio-technical dimensions of information systems, with particular emphasis on cybersecurity and privacy, digital leadership, entrepreneurship, and technology-enabled organizational transformation. My investigations examine how emerging technologies shape individual and organizational behaviors, decision-making processes, and strategic outcomes in contemporary digital environments.

Methodologically, my research employs rigorous quantitative and computational approaches, including longitudinal and cross-sectional survey designs, experimental methods, structural equation modeling, econometric analysis, machine learning applications, natural language processing, large language models, bibliometric analysis, systematic literature reviews, and meta-analytic techniques. This methodological versatility enables comprehensive examination of complex phenomena across multiple levels of analysis.

My scholarly contributions have appeared in peer-reviewed journals such as Computers in Human Behavior, European Journal of Innovation Management, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, and SAGE Open. My work has also been presented at premier information systems conferences including the International Conference on Information Systems (ICIS) and the Pacific Asia Conference on Information Systems (PACIS).

I serve the academic community as an ad-hoc reviewer for distinguished journals including Information Systems Frontiers, Technological Forecasting & Social Change, The International Journal of Management Education, and Asian Education and Development Studies. I also contribute as a reviewer for leading conferences such as the Hawaii International Conference on System Sciences (HICSS) and the Australasian Conference on Information Systems (ACIS).

This platform serves as a repository for my research activities, publications, and ongoing scholarly contributions. Inquiries regarding research collaboration and academic partnerships are welcome.
