Research Interests

My research investigates the intersection of technology and organizational phenomena, focusing on:

  • Information Systems and Behavior: Examining how individuals and organizations interact with digital systems and the behavioral implications of technology adoption and use.
  • Cybersecurity and Privacy: Investigating organizational preparedness, risk management strategies, and the socio-technical dimensions of digital security.
  • Digital Leadership: Exploring leadership practices in technology-mediated environments and their impact on organizational outcomes.
  • Digital Entrepreneurship: Analyzing entrepreneurial processes, intentions, and ecosystem dynamics in digital contexts.

Research Methodologies

My research program employs diverse methodological approaches to ensure rigorous investigation of complex phenomena:

  • Survey Research: Cross-sectional and longitudinal studies examining organizational and individual-level phenomena.
  • Data Collection: Survey, Web scraping, data mining, and automated data extraction from digital platforms.
  • Experimental Design: Controlled experiments to establish causal relationships and test theoretical mechanisms.
  • Statistical Modeling: Structural equation modeling (SEM), econometric analysis, and advanced regression techniques.
  • Computational Methods: Machine learning, natural language processing (NLP), large language models (LLMs), and text analytics.
  • Systematic Reviews: Bibliometric analysis, meta-analysis, and systematic literature reviews following established protocols.
